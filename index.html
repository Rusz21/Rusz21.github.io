Theoretical Effect on Training

A small learning rate leads to slow but stable convergence, while a large learning rate can speed up training but risks overshooting the optimal solution. Choosing an appropriate learning rate is critical for both convergence and generalization.

Results and Observations

The experiment revealed that:

  • Very small learning rates converged slowly but were stable.
  • Moderate learning rates achieved faster convergence and better accuracy.
  • Large learning rates caused unstable training and fluctuating loss.

These results highlight the importance of tuning the learning rate carefully. A well-chosen learning rate can significantly improve training efficiency and model performance.

Key Takeaways

From this experiment, I learned that the learning rate directly affects convergence speed, stability, and final model accuracy. In future projects, I plan to use learning rate scheduling and adaptive optimizers to further improve training performance.
